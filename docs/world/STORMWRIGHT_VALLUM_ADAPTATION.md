# Stormwright Vallum Adaptation Plan

## Purpose

This document defines how The Stormwright Cycle becomes playable inside Vallum Engine.

The adaptation should respect the literary intent of the source world while translating it into systems: scenes, routes, party state, moral pressure, dice checks, consequences, and replayable choices.

## Adaptation boundary

The source series is the master canon. Vallum is not rewriting the novels. Vallum is creating playable side-structures and vertical slices that let the player experience the world through bounded decisions.

The game version can compress, reframe, or stage scenes differently for playability, but it must not flatten the core themes into generic combat fantasy.

## Core adaptation pillars

### 1. Violence is capability, not identity

The player should often be capable of force. The question is whether force clarifies or corrupts the situation.

### 2. Restraint is active

Not acting is not the same as doing nothing. The engine should support choices where restraint creates information, cost, delay, or future trust.

### 3. The world reads reputation before intent

Kael may reject the name Iron Captain, but other people still use it. The player should experience the gap between self-understanding and public meaning.

### 4. Witness matters

Seeing accurately is a mechanic. Some scenes should reward asking, observing, documenting, waiting, or letting another person speak.

### 5. Mythology arrives through pressure

The Stormwright, the Turning, the Ven Codex, and the Old Faith should enter through lived consequence, not lore dump.

## Proposed Stormwright-specific mechanics

### Force

Measures immediate capacity to impose an outcome. Used for combat, command, intimidation, crisis action, and tactical control.

### Restraint

Measures capacity to hold force in reserve. Used when the obvious action is not necessarily the right action.

### Witness

Measures capacity to see accurately and accept what is seen. Used for investigation, moral recognition, reading people, and surviving self-deception.

### Hollow

Tracks psychic and moral cost. Hollow rises when Kael acts without asking, ignores evidence, uses force as certainty, or performs identity rather than choosing action. Hollow may also rise when he chooses correctly but cannot avoid cost.

### Reputation

Tracks how others read Kael. Reputation can open doors, close doors, frighten people, attract commissions, distort testimony, or create pressure to become what others expect.

## v0.3 playable module: The Noise of Purpose

### Module intent

Adapt the emotional and tactical structure of Book I Chapter 1 into a short playable Vallum session.

The module should test whether Vallum can support a serious Stormwright scene: pressure, violence, choice, consequence, and the beginning of hollow.

### Player role

The player controls Kael and may later control supporting figures, but the first version should keep party complexity low.

Possible party model:

- Kael Vorn as primary playable character
- two unnamed or lightly named soldiers as tactical state markers
- civilians as protected objective tokens rather than party members

### Scene structure

Scene 1: Ridge Above the Caravan

The player sees the burning caravan below, raiders moving through smoke, civilians exposed, and the raider captain acting as the tactical centre.

Core choice tension: save civilians now or cut the head of the attack.

Scene 2A: Cut the Head

Kael attacks the captain. Force check. Success may scatter the raiders but cost civilians. Failure creates injury and chaos.

Scene 2B: Protect the Civilians

Kael moves to shield or extract civilians. Restraint or tactical check. Success saves lives but lets the captain reposition. Failure splits attention.

Scene 2C: Command the Men

Kael uses reputation and command presence. Reputation or Force check. Success coordinates both priorities imperfectly. Failure exposes the limits of authority under fire.

Scene 2D: Observe Before Acting

Kael spends a moment reading the attack pattern. Witness check. Success reveals the captain's actual objective. Failure costs time.

Scene 3: The Surge

The engine presents the surge not as a power-up but as narrowing. The world becomes clearer and less human at the same time.

Scene 4: Aftermath

The module records what was saved, what was lost, what Kael felt, and what he refused to name.

### End states

End state A: Tactical victory, civilian cost

Kael kills or breaks the captain, but civilians die or are abandoned.

End state B: Civilian rescue, threat escaped

Lives are saved, but the captain survives and the conflict continues.

End state C: Imperfect command

The party achieves partial success but Kael's reputation grows in a way that may later trap him.

End state D: Witnessed truth

Kael sees more than others do, but seeing does not make the choice easier.

## Required engine extensions for v0.3

The current v0.2 engine can support the basic flow but needs several additions for Stormwright quality:

- non-party objective state, such as civilians saved or lost
- moral state values, especially Hollow and Reputation
- outcome labels beyond success or failure
- scene-specific stakes panel
- support for single-character modules
- module metadata for source note and canon status

## Visual direction for v0.3

The map should shift from road-node fantasy to battlefield tableau:

- ridge position
- burning caravan
- civilian cluster
- raider captain
- smoke or fire markers
- extraction route

The player should feel that the map is an ethical layout, not just a tactical diagram.

## Writing direction

Narration should remain close to Kael's perception: terrain first, feeling indirectly, action precise, emotion delayed.

Do not over-explain the moral question. Stage the facts and let the choice expose the cost.

## Success criteria

v0.3 succeeds if Phil can play The Noise of Purpose and feel that Kael's world has entered the engine without becoming generic fantasy.

The player should finish the module with a record of what was done, what was saved, what was lost, and what began inside Kael.
