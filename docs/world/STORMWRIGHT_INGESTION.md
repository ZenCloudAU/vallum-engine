## Protagonist

**Name in books**: Vaen (later. Series bible notes KAEL as alternative — this is why game uses Kael Vorn)
**Game name**: Kael Vorn, Iron Captain

**Origin**: Third son of minor landholder, eastern marches. No inheritance. Sword at fifteen.

**The Surge**: Heightened operational state. World narrows, action effortless, time moves differently. Not supernatural (as far as he knows). Series reveals it as Ardent resonance — he is one of the most strongly touched individuals alive.

**The Hollow**: What follows the surge. A void. He has spent his life either chasing the surge to avoid the hollow, or learning to sit inside the hollow without flinching. The series is about the second education.

**Core wound**: Reshen. Valley cleared on Caeden's false commission. Children who did not run fast enough. A miller named Fen who died hiding three of them under floorboards.

**Core question**: Can a man be defined by his worst act — and if not, what defines him instead?

## NPC Voice Reference (for GM prompt and future writing)

### Lord Caeden
- Never raises voice. Measured. Deliberate.
- Offers wine before the commission.
- Language: "sympathy", "shelter", "passage", "the valley as a corridor"
- Never mentions violence directly. The instrument is implied, not named.
- Key line: "It's less about fighters than about the shelter itself."

### Sera
- Practical, self-possessed, entirely unafraid of him.
- Listens without performance — no careful expressions, no managed distance.
- Loved the fire. Waited for the heat to return.
- Key exchange: "You're not here." / "I am here." / "Whoever is sitting there is not the man I know."
- Her verdict is not unkind. It is more final than unkind.

### Mael (Caeden's intermediary)
- Smooth administrative confidence.
- Carried the commission knowing what it meant.
- Key line: "I knew what was necessary. You knew too. You didn't ask."
- Does not fight. Negotiates. Makes culpability sound shared.

### Edric (cartographer)
- Small, dry, precise. Reduced to essentials through long practice.
- Arrives discharging an obligation he did not choose.
- Key line: "I have run out of methods for living with it quietly."
- Found one of the Reshen children, now a young woman, years later.
- Miller's name: Fen. Died hiding three children. They survived.

### Davan
- Fair-haired, lean, nineteen. Stillness not performance.
- Key exchange: "How many men have you killed?" / "Four." / "How did it feel?" / "Clean. Like a question answered."
- He is Kael's past, still walking around.
- Reappears (Book II Ch.VIII) three years later, now with the hollow.

### Calla (from Wrenford)
- Perhaps forty. Village woman. Dress was good once, maintained.
- Not afraid of him. Did not adjust when she saw him.
- Describes the situation without performance. Stops when she has said it.
- Key line: "I came here expecting to have tried."
- Torven (collector): third son, minor appointment, unauthorised fees.

### Raider Captain (Ch.I)
- Professional. Unhurried. Raised his axe in salute — inviting, not mocking.
- Key moment: their eyes meet and the man smiled. Kael felt something he had heard before.
- His smile never fully left his face.

## Moral Mechanics — What Each Stat Means in the Books

| Stat | Book meaning |
|---|---|
| **Force** | The Surge. The heat that rises fast and narrows the world. Seductive because it feels like clarity. |
| **Restraint** | The gap. The ability to stand inside the heat without acting. Grows through specific failures. |
| **Witness** | The cartographer's eye. Seeing what is there, including oneself. Edric's discipline. The account. |
| **Hollow** | The cost. What follows the Surge. Grows when choices feed the heat for its own sake. |
| **Reputation** | The Iron Captain. The name that precedes him. Useful. Also a cage. |

## Hollow Thresholds (for GM narration tone)

- **0–3**: Contained. The usual tiredness after action.
- **4–6**: Opening. He knows it is there. Has not decided what to do with knowing.
- **7–9**: Rising. Something moves in the space where a more complete person used to stand.
- **10**: The account closes. The hollow has become the whole of him.

## Themes for Future Act/Scene Writing

From the Series Bible:

1. **Witnessing as moral action** — Edric's folio, the miller's name, the chronicle itself. To see accurately without the instinct to justify is the rarest discipline.

2. **The difference between peace as absence and peace as refusal** — Stated first in the prologue, tested across five books. Coming down the hill without the surge is not the same as not coming down the hill.

3. **What we were and what we are** — The past does not disappear. It becomes the weight we carry or the ground we stand on.

4. **The ledger** — Justice as a fiction the world enforces selectively. Caeden dies of a fever. The world does not balance its books.

5. **The Storm motif** — The series' organising image. Present in every act. In Book V: the storm and the man watching it are no longer metaphor.

## Acts II–IV Material (Books III–V) — Future Game Expansion

If the game expands beyond Book I:

**Book II** (Withdrawal): Davan returns with the hollow. Maren begins observing from a distance.

**Book III** (Conscription): Political crisis in the Heartlands. The Reform Faction. Esvar (the Thorn). Mael reappears in authority. Maren makes contact. Mythological layer becomes legible.

**Book IV** (Revelation): The Wardens explain. Ardent vs Verant cosmology. Kael accepts some and argues the rest. Kael of the Harren (POV character) arrives.

**Book V** (Resolution): Kael must witness a specific event with sufficient accuracy that the observation constitutes a record. The record has the force of a treaty. Young Aldric Vane asks a question. Kael answers it.
