# Stormwright Canon Guide

## Canon status

The Stormwright Cycle series bible is now the master world guide for Vallum Engine's first original content line.

This guide does not replace the uploaded source document. It is the game-design extraction layer: the part of the canon that Vallum needs in order to build modules, scenes, character sheets, choices, map routes, faction pressure, and rules abstractions.

## Adaptation principle

Stormwright is not to be treated as generic fantasy. The core experience is not power escalation, loot acquisition, or heroic destiny. It is moral pressure, restraint, force, consequence, political instability, and the cost of becoming capable of violence.

The game should let the player inhabit difficult decisions rather than simply win encounters.

## Central playable question

What does it cost a person to become capable of great violence, and what does it cost them to stop using that capacity as identity?

In game terms, the player should repeatedly face choices between capability, restraint, obligation, withdrawal, reputation, and consequence.

## Primary world frame

The early Vallum adaptation should begin in the Compact Lands.

The Compact Lands are a loose confederation created after the Sundering. They contain interior lordships, eastern march garrisons, southern free cities, northern territories, and eventually the Reach. The political order is stable enough to function but too fragmented to hold under deep pressure.

For v0.3, the playable geography should stay narrow. Use one road, one garrison or settlement, one threatened community, and one moral pressure point. The wider Compact should be felt, not explained.

## Major territories for future modules

### Interior Lordships

Stable, agricultural, administrative, insulated from border violence. Good for political modules, petitions, corruption, quiet coercion, lordly pressure, and reputational consequences.

### Eastern March Garrisons

Military frontier culture. Good for early Kael formation modules, contract violence, chain of command, tactical choices, and moral deflection.

### Southern Free Cities

Trade, debt, independence, and later political expansion. Good for Book III and later modules involving Sera, trade power, and coastal theology.

### Northern Territories

Ungoverned or lightly governed lands. Good for withdrawal, stone chamber material, road decisions, weather, silence, and the boundary between hiding and choosing.

### The Reach

Ven territory and the source of the Ven Codex. Reserved for later mythological modules. It should not be introduced too early as exotic discovery. It is a fully developed society with its own history, politics, and claims.

## Theology and mythic layer

The Old Faith reads patterns in the world rather than demanding ordinary worship. Priests and scholars interpret weather, rivers, choices, lives, and historical rupture.

The Aethers are six divine forces whose withdrawal is called the First Silence.

The Ven Codex describes cycles, Turnings, and the Stormwright pattern.

The Turning is not a simple apocalypse. It is a shift in cycle that produces political instability, theological conflict, and personal pressure.

The Stormwright is not a chosen hero. The pattern describes a threshold figure shaped by accumulated choices. This distinction is central to the game.

## Core protagonist model

Kael Vorn is not a conventional hero. He is a man whose identity became legible through competence at violence.

He notices terrain, tactical shape, silence, threat, and movement before he names emotion.

His game representation should prioritise capability, restraint, reputation, hollow, witness, and consequence. Do not reduce him to a generic fighter class.

Suggested Vallum archetype: Iron Captain.

Core stats for early modules:

- Force: capacity to act decisively under threat
- Restraint: capacity not to use force when force is available
- Witness: capacity to see accurately without self-protection
- Hollow: accumulated psychic cost of action, inaction, and moral evasion
- Reputation: how the world reads him, whether or not he accepts the name

## Key supporting characters

### Edric Lenn

Cartographer. Precise, morally serious, compromised by what his maps enabled. Best used in modules involving evidence, routes, documents, administrative violence, and the difference between accuracy and complicity.

### Sera

Not a failed romance or villain. She loved something real in Kael but not the whole of him. Best used later as mirror, trade actor, and evidence of chosen life outside his path.

### Davan

Young soldier, parallel study in capability and hollow. Best used as a playable contrast to Kael: what happens when the question is faced earlier.

### Calla

Practical petitioner, adult equal, community pressure. Best used when the correct action may be a letter, a route, a witness, or a decision rather than a sword.

### Sister Maret

Scholar of the Witnessing. Interprets lives against theology. Best used as the bridge between intimate story and Stormwright pattern.

## Antagonist model

Stormwright antagonists should not begin as monsters. The early product line should favour competent people using systems, reputation, distance, and delegated force.

Lord Caeden is the model: administrative competence, convenience, plausible language, and clean hands.

The Triumvirate later represents order at scale and the cost imposed on communities.

Veth later represents competing interpretation, not cartoon evil.

## Module design tone

Stormwright modules should feel deliberate, restrained, and morally pressurised.

The best playable actions are not simply attack, defend, flee, talk. They are choices such as:

- cut the head or protect the civilians
- ask the question or accept the commission
- write the letter or take up the sword
- withdraw or hide
- witness or intervene
- preserve order or expose cost
- act with certainty or act without certainty

## Visual and UX direction

The Vallum UI should lean into maps, roads, chambers, storms, candlelight, documents, weather, and tactical stillness.

The board should not feel arcade-like. Movement should feel like consequence. Each route should imply time, weather, exposure, and obligation.

Character sheets should show moral state as much as combat state.

## First adaptation target

v0.3 should not attempt Book I at full scale.

The first module should adapt the emotional structure of Book I Chapter 1: the ridge above the burning caravan, the choice between saving civilians and cutting the head, the surge, the raider captain, and the cost of purpose.

Working module title:

The Noise of Purpose

This module should be short, playable, and designed to test whether Vallum can render Stormwright's real tone.
